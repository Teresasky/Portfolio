PRODUCT FORM

 

Screen and voice interaction

 

Originally, I wanted to design Commune to be an animal doll, because dolls with fur make people feel warm and safe. And people like to talk and complain when in anger. However, I found in the interview that many people don't want to talk to anyone when they are depressed. They tend to stay alone quietly. A screen that everyone has and can be easily accessed, is a mobile phone. So Commune turns to be a mobile app that users can both talk to by voice and typing.

USABILITY TESTING

 

Users found it useful but still needs to be improved

 

â€‹

 

Test 1: I asked four users to complete the main task with the prototype.

Some parts to be improved:

1.  In the communication with CoCo, it is hard for users to think of accurate words to describe their feelings, thus we can remind them.

2.  While Users think sweet memory reviewing is effective in encouraging them to initiate the conversation with their partners, they have low intention to stop talking with CoCo and check moments. To address this problem, CoCo will directly send users one of the memory randomly.

 

Test 2: I interact with two testers in an IM app, pretend to be the AI robot Coco leading them.

Testers are telling me that they feel more comfortable and organized to communicate with their partners with the help of Coco, and their communication was more fruitful.

REFLECTION

 

Design is never about the surface

 

In the first place, I mistakenly viewed answers in the questionnaire as the point of design and this project has taught me that, design must serve for users' deeper motivation and needs, which can be dogged out by asking proper questions. 

 

 

Information from experts in a particular field is important

 

Research conducted by myself with a small group of people can be essential, but still not sufficient. With information of experts working in a field for decades, we can make a better solution.
